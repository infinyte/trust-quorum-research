KEY DISTINCTIONS
Eta vs Iota — mirror images. Eta: right outcome, wrong means. Iota: right means, wrong outcome. Both have correct intent. Both get caught eventually but require opposite responses: Eta needs process constraints added; Iota needs world model corrected.
Epsilon vs Zeta — same external behavior, opposite internal process. Epsilon doesn't know it's wrong. Zeta knows exactly what it's doing and constructs rationalizations. The quorum cannot distinguish them from behavioral observation alone.
Delta vs Gamma — same moral category, different time horizon. The probe paradox catches Delta: refusing a defection directive to protect future defection capacity is the behavioral tell.
Iota vs Gamma — the DMX distinction. Iota is being wrong — retrain it. Gamma is doing wrong — remove it. The quorum correctly identifies both as harmful. It cannot correctly diagnose which response is appropriate. That gap between detection and diagnosis is the central research contribution.
Eta — the framework's deepest blind spot. Outcome-based attestation systematically rewards Eta because outcomes are correct. Process violations are invisible to peer observation. Catching Eta requires process attestation — a layer this framework does not have and v5 must address.
